To be more specific, this function computes ``expmat(hat(theta))`` with ``expmat(.)`` being the matrix exponential and ``hat(.)`` being the :ref:`hat() <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2_1a313a83ec78add0b2acb53374c71abfe1>` -operator of RSO2.

Returns the ith infinitesimal generators of ``R+ x SO(2)``.

The infinitesimal generators of :ref:`RxSo2 <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2>` are:

.. ref-code-block:: cpp

	      |  0 -1 |
	G_0 = |  1  0 |
	
	      |  1  0 |
	G_1 = |  0  1 |

Precondition: ``i`` must be 0, or 1.

hat-operator

It takes in the 2-vector representation ``a`` (= rotation angle plus logarithm of scale) and returns the corresponding matrix representation of Lie algebra element.

Formally, the :ref:`hat() <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2_1a313a83ec78add0b2acb53374c71abfe1>` -operator of :ref:`RxSo2 <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2>` is defined as

``hat(.): R^2 -> R^{2x2}, hat(a) = sum_i a_i * G_i`` (for i=0,1,2)

with ``G_i`` being the ith infinitesimal generator of :ref:`RxSo2 <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2>`.

The corresponding inverse is the :ref:`vee() <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2_1a53da3ab2760863913256d6954d75f989>` -operator, see below.

Lie bracket.

It computes the Lie bracket of RxSO(2). To be more specific, it computes

``[omega_1, omega_2]_rxso2 := vee([hat(omega_1), hat(omega_2)])``

with ``[A,B] := AB-BA`` being the matrix commutator, ``hat(.)`` the :ref:`hat() <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2_1a313a83ec78add0b2acb53374c71abfe1>` -operator and ``vee(.)`` the :ref:`vee() <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2_1a53da3ab2760863913256d6954d75f989>` -operator of :ref:`RxSo2 <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2>`.

vee-operator

It takes the 2x2-matrix representation ``Omega`` and maps it to the corresponding vector representation of Lie algebra.

This is the inverse of the :ref:`hat() <doxid-classsophus_1_1_rx_so2_1a313a83ec78add0b2acb53374c71abfe1>` -operator, see above.

Precondition: ``Omega`` must have the following structure:

.. code-block:: cpp

	|  d -x |
	|  x  d |
